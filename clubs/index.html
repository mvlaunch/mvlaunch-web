MIT Launch Clubs

MIT Launch provides materials and support to high schools throughout the school year to allow students to develop entrepreneurial skills and mindset by launching startups. Club members start a company in teams, using course materials that follow the rigor and standards of MIT. This is not a business plan contest. The aim is to create a real startup â€“ to solve a real need in a differentiated way. The focus is on taking action, learning from results, and making changes to improve chances of success.

Join us and our network of educators in changing the way students learn and solve problems. We ask for a champion of a high school (a student or teacher) spearhead development of the club to implement the materials and recruit members, plus attend webinars to learn implementation of our materials. Apply to start a club at your school below.

Resources

We have a world class learning structure. Students are provided resources to enable them to build their entrepreneurial toolkit.

Materials

Course materials and activities are provided to club organizers through an online course platform, plus taught through frequent webinars.

Coaching

Teams are given a network of mentors with a wealth of business and entrepreneurship experience to help guide and support them throughout the startup process.

Network

Launch provides forums and conferences to all clubs members, plus a final mentorship and pitch event held at MIT for the finalist teams.
